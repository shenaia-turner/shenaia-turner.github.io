Pillion

Project Pillion focuses on clay deposition printing by optimizing robotic toolpaths to create ceramic interlocking bricks. Machine learning was integrated to enhance material handling and printing accuracy.

BOX-LAMINATIONS

The objective of BOX-LAMINATIONS is to use the properties of different woods and the affordances of digital fabrication as design and form generators. The model of our wood is split into strips and irregular pieces were grouped from single and double curved shapes to separate the more planar pieces from the curvy pieces.

AIRFORMATICS

This project is a collaborative research undertaken during a one-semester, 16-week studio at the University of Pennsylvania as part of the Master of Science and Design in Robotics and Autonomous Systems program. We asked the question: Could the inflation of thermoplastic preforms be a suitable manufacturing method to create thin-walled high-volume architectural components? To address this, an automated manufacturing approach aimed at enhancing scale and inflation formation for the Fused Granular Fabrication (FGF) based production is used in combination with an inflation simulation scenario and a custom end-of-arm tool for inflation.
